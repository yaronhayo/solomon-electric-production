## The EV Charger Installation Process

### Step 1: Site Assessment
- Evaluate panel capacity (need 40-50 amps available)
- Determine charger location and circuit route
- Calculate installation costs

### Step 2: Permit Application
Submit electrical permit to Miami-Dade or Broward building departmentâ€”we handle all paperwork.

### Step 3: Panel Preparation
If panel lacks capacity, upgrade service before charger installation.

### Step 4: Circuit Installation
- Run 6-gauge or 4-gauge wire from panel to charger location
- Install dedicated 40-60 amp circuit breaker
- Proper grounding and GFCI protection

### Step 5: Charger Installation
- Mount Wall Connector or install NEMA 14-50 outlet
- Connect wiring and test
- Configure smart features (scheduling, load management)

### Step 6: Inspection & Activation
Building department inspection, then activate charger for use.

**Timeline**: 1-3 days depending on permit processing and panel work needed.

## Choosing Your Charging Solution

### Tesla Wall Connector
**Best for**: Tesla owners, hardwired installation, fastest charging (up to 48 amps)  
**Features**: WiFi connectivity, power sharing, Tesla app integration  
**Cost**: Higher upfront but fastest charging speeds

### Universal Level 2 Charger (Hardwired)
**Best for**: Non-Tesla EVs, permanent installation  
**Features**: Works with all EVs, 32-40 amp charging  
**Cost**: Mid-range pricing, excellent compatibility

### NEMA 14-50 Outlet
**Best for**: Portable charger flexibility, multiple vehicle types  
**Features**: Use any portable Level 2 charger, easy to upgrade later  
**Cost**: Most affordable installation

### Commercial Charging Stations
**Best for**: Businesses, apartments, multi-family properties  
**Features**: Load management, billing integration, multiple units  
**Cost**: Higher investment, revenue potential

## Miami-Specific Considerations

### Climate Factors
- Outdoor charger installations need weather-resistant equipment
- South Florida heat affects charging efficiency
- Hurricane preparation for outdoor charging equipment

### Utility Incentives
Check FPL (Florida Power & Light) for potential rebates on EV charger installation and time-of-use rate programs.

### HOA & Permitting
- Condo and HOA approvals often required
- Miami-Dade and Broward permit requirements
- We navigate all approval processes

### Future-Proofing
Install higher-capacity circuits (60A vs. 40A) for faster charging as EV battery sizes increase.
